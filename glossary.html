A

Absolute Return

Absolute Return

It measures performance of a fund over any two points in time, of any length say 1-Year, 3-Year, 5-Year, 10-Year etc.

Alpha

Alpha

A measure of risk-adjusted return. Positive alpha indicates that a security has performed better than it’s benchmark.

Asset Allocation

Asset Allocation

Division of an investment among different asset classes such as equity, fixed income, cash, gold, real estate etc. It depends on the time horizon, goal and ability to tolerate risk.

Asset Class

Alpha

Category of different investment types, such as equity, fixed income, real estate, cash, gold etc.

Average Maturity

Average Maturity

Average Maturity is the average of different maturities of the fixed income securities, which make up a debt mutual fund’s portfolio.

B

Benchmark

Benchmark

A suitable index against which the performance of the fund is compared. For example, equity funds benchmark against the Sensex or Nifty index.

Beta

Beta

The indicated level of volatility associated with the fund as compared to its benchmark, for e.g. comparing a fund with Nifty/Sensex. A beta of 1 means that the fund's performance closely matches the benchmark. A beta that is greater than 1 is more volatile than the benchmark Index. A beta that is less than 1 is less volatile than the benchmark Index.

C

Close Ended Scheme

Close Ended Scheme

A close-ended fund or scheme has a fixed maturity period.

Cost Averaging

Cost Averaging

A strategy that involves buying units of the fund at regular intervals of time rather than buying at one go. This allows investors to take advantage of increase and decrease in the price. Purchases at low prices offset the purchase made at high prices allowing investors to hold the investment at a reasonable average cost. Within mutual funds, it's a strategy used by systematic investment plans(SIP).

D

Diversification

Diversification

Diversification is the process of balancing the asset classes in a portfolio in a way which reduce exposure to risk.

E

Equity Linked Savings Scheme

Equity Linked Savings Scheme

A special product offered by mutual funds which gives investors the option of saving tax while investing into diversified equity mutual funds.

Exit Load

Exit Load

Exit load is the charge you pay the fund house, if you wish to exit the scheme before a stipulated period. It is levied on a few categories/schemes where investors are expected to stay invested for a stipulated time period so as to minimize the risk.

Expense Ratio

Expense Ratio

Expense ratio is the annual percentage of a fund’s assets that are paid out for expenses such as management fee and any other fee that is spent for the fund’s operation.

F

Folio number

Folio number

A unique account number, akin to a bank account number given by the fund house to you. By quoting your folio number, you can get a list of your unit holdings with the fund houses.

Fund Manager

Fund Manager

A Fund Manager has the expertise to manage an investor’s money in line with the Mutual Fund’s stated investment objectives like income generation, capital appreciation etc.

Fund of funds

Fund of funds

Schemes that instead of investing in stocks and bonds, provide the same exposure indirectly by investing in other mutual fund schemes.

I

Index fund

Index fund

A scheme whose portfolio mirrors an Index, both in terms of composition and individual stock weightages. It's a passive investment option, as a fund's performance will mimic the index concerned barring a minor tracking error.

L

Liquidity

Liquidity

An asset is said to be liquid when it can be sold in the market quickly for cash.

Liquidity Risk

Liquidity Risk

Risk that the marketplace will not have enough buyers when an investor seeks to sell a security.

Lock-in period

Lock-in period

The period for which investments made in a scheme cannot be withdrawn. In mutual funds, a lock-in period of three years is applicable on equity-linked savings schemes.

N

Net Asset Value (NAV)

Net Asset Value (NAV)

Net Asset Value is the worth for each unit of the fund. It is calculated as the market value of all assets less liabilities and expenses divided by the outstanding number of units in the fund.

O

Open-ended fund

Open-ended fund

A scheme that investors can enter and exit at any point of time. This convenience gives it the edge over close-ended schemes, and makes it the preferred option, for investors.

S

Standard deviation

Standard deviation

A statistical measure of the extent of variance of a fund's performance. A fund is said to have a higher standard deviation when its performance deviates widely; this indicates that there is a greater potential for volatility.

Sharpe Ratio

Sharpe Ratio

A ratio to measure risk-adjusted performance. The Sharpe ratio tells us whether the returns of a portfolio are due to good investment decisions or a result of excess risk

Systematic Investment Plan (SIP)

Systematic Investment Plan (SIP)

SIP allows an investor to invest a predetermined amount in a scheme at set intervals and derive the benefit of fluctuating NAVs. So, when the NAV drops, the investor gets more units and when the NAV moves up, he gets less.

Systematic Transfer Plan (STP)

Systematic Transfer Plan (STP)

STP allows an investor to transfer a predetermined amount from one scheme to another scheme in the same fund house at set intervals.

Systematic Withdrawal Plan (SWP)

Systematic Withdrawal Plan (SWP)

A payment plan that lets you withdraw predetermined amounts from your investments from a scheme at periodic intervals.

T

Tracking error

Tracking error

The difference between the returns generated by an Index fund and the Index it tracks. Tracking error arises because even an Index fund incurs various expenses like fund management fee, brokerage and agents commission.

U

Unit

Unit

Just like an investor buys shares of a company, he buys units of a mutual fund. A unit is nothing more than a share in a mutual fund scheme.

Y

Yield to Maturity (YTM)

Yield to Maturity (YTM)

Yield to Maturity is the expected yield on an investment if it is held by the investor till its maturity date.
